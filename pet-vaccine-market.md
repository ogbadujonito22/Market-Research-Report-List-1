<p><ul><li>Attenuated Live Vaccines</li><li>Conjugate Vaccines</li><li>Inactivated Vaccines</li><li>Subunit Vaccines</li><li>Toxoid Vaccines</li><li>DNA Vaccines</li><li>Recombinant Vaccines</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The pet vaccine market comprises several types, each serving distinct purposes. Attenuated live vaccines use weakened pathogens to stimulate immunity. Inactivated vaccines contain killed pathogens, prompting a safe immune response. Conjugate vaccines link antigens to enhance effectiveness, while subunit vaccines include only specific parts of a pathogen. Toxoid vaccines target toxins produced by bacteria. DNA vaccines introduce genetic material to produce an immune response, and recombinant vaccines use modified viruses or bacteria to deliver antigens, enhancing protection for pets.</p></p>
