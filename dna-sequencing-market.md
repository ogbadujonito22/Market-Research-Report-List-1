<p><p>The DNA sequencing market is categorized into three generations. First-generation sequencing, including Sanger sequencing, is characterized by high accuracy but low throughput and is primarily used for smaller projects. Second-generation sequencing, or next-generation sequencing (NGS), offers massively parallel sequencing, enabling rapid and cost-effective analysis for larger genomic studies. Third-generation sequencing allows for real-time sequencing of single molecules, providing longer reads and better insights into complex genomic regions, facilitating advances in personalized medicine and various biological applications.</p></p>
