<p><p>Gas suppression fire systems are crucial for fire protection in environments where water damage from traditional extinguishing methods is a concern. Inert gas fire suppression systems utilize gases like nitrogen or argon to reduce oxygen levels, effectively suppressing fires without harmful residues. Chemical fire suppression systems, on the other hand, use specialized agents that interrupt the chemical reaction of a fire. Both types serve specific applications, with inert gases favored in sensitive electronic areas and chemicals in industrial settings.</p></p>
