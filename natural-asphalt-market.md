<p><p>The natural asphalt market can be categorized into two main types: derived from coal and derived from oil. Coal-derived asphalt, often known as coal tar pitch, is a byproduct of coal carbonization and is used in applications such as road construction and roofing. On the other hand, oil-derived asphalt, produced through the refining of crude oil, is more prevalent in paving and waterproofing industries. Both types serve essential roles in infrastructure development, but their properties and applications differ significantly.</p></p>
